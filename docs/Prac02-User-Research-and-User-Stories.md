Practical 2 Report
Project Title: Hello Dear – Anonymous Student Peer Support Platform
1. Introduction
Hello Dear is a web-based anonymous peer support platform designed for university students. The platform allows students to share academic pressure, friendship problems, emotional concerns and university-life experiences without publicly revealing their real identities. Other students can provide encouragement, practical suggestions and supportive responses.
The platform is designed as a peer-support community rather than a professional medical or counselling service. Its main purpose is to create a safer and more comfortable space where students from different academic programmes can communicate and support one another.
Task One: Target User Interviews

2. Target Users

The target users of Hello Dear are university students from different programmes.
Example：
Human Resource Management students may experience pressure from group presentations, communication activities, internships and interpersonal relationships.
Accounting students may experience pressure caused by examinations, calculations, assignment deadlines and concerns about academic performance.
Information Technology students may experience difficulties related to programming assignments, technical problems, group projects and long periods of independent computer work.
3. Interview Method
The proposed interview group consists of six university students. Two students are from Human Resource Management, two are from Accounting and two are from Information Technology. Participants should be identified as Participant 1 to Participant 6 instead of using their real names.
The interviews can be conducted face-to-face, through online calls or through text messages. Each interview should take approximately ten to fifteen minutes. Participants should be informed that their answers will only be used to identify software requirements and that their names will not be published.

4. Interview Questions
1.	What types of pressure or difficulties do you normally experience as a university student?
2.	When you feel stressed, are you comfortable discussing your problems with other people?
3.	Would anonymity make you feel more comfortable sharing your concerns?
4.	Would you use a platform that allows students to support one another anonymously?
5.	What types of posts would you like to read or publish on the platform?
6.	What features would make the platform useful and easy to use?
7.	Would you like to comment on other students’ posts or use simple supportive reactions?
8.	What privacy concerns would you have when using an anonymous platform?
9.	What should the platform do when harmful or inappropriate content is posted?
10.	Would you like the platform to provide links to university support services?

5.Summary of Representative User Feedback

The Human Resource Management students indicated that interpersonal relationships and group assignments were important sources of pressure. They believed that students might be more willing to discuss friendship conflicts, communication difficulties and internship concerns if their real names were not displayed.
They also suggested that posts should be divided into clear categories. Suggested categories included academic pressure, friendship, internship, campus life and general support. They believed that categories would help users quickly find relevant discussions.
The Accounting students explained that examinations, difficult calculations and assignment deadlines were common sources of stress. They were interested in reading posts from students who had experienced similar academic difficulties.
They preferred short and supportive responses rather than judgmental comments. They suggested reactions such as “I understand”, “You are not alone” and “Sending support”. They also wanted the ability to save useful posts and return to them later.
The Information Technology students focused more strongly on platform security and technical usability. They wanted the system to avoid displaying real names, student identification numbers, personal email addresses and phone numbers.
They also believed that users should be able to report inappropriate posts and comments. An administrator should review reported content and remove material that violates the platform’s community guidelines.
Students from all three programmes agreed that anonymous posting was the most important function. They also considered anonymous commenting, post categories, content reporting, moderation and mobile-friendly design to be important requirements.
Several students expressed concerns that complete anonymity could encourage harmful behaviour. Therefore, the platform should hide users’ identities from other students while still using internal account information to prevent repeated violations.
Students also wanted a support-resource page containing university counselling information, student services and other official support channels. However, they agreed that this information should not interrupt normal discussions unless it was relevant.

6. Identified Requirements
Based on the user feedback, the platform must allow students to create an account while using an anonymous display name. Their real identity should not be visible to other users.
Students must be able to create anonymous posts containing a title, description and category. They must also be able to view posts published by other students.
Students must be able to report posts or comments that are harmful, insulting, discriminatory, misleading or unrelated to the purpose of the platform.
Administrators must be able to review reported content, remove inappropriate posts and comments, and take action against users who repeatedly violate community guidelines.
Students should be able to edit or delete their own posts. They should also be able to save useful posts and receive notifications when another user responds to their content.
The platform should include a page containing official university support resources. The system must also protect personal information and provide a clear, simple and mobile-friendly interface.

Task Two: User Stories
7. Priority and Effort Definition
Priority 10 represents a critical function that must be included in the first working version of the platform.
Priority 20 represents a high-priority function that should be completed soon after the critical functions.
Priority 30 represents a medium-priority function that improves usability but is not essential for the first version.
Priority 40 represents a low-priority function that may be developed in a later version.
Priority 50 represents a future enhancement that will only be developed if sufficient time and resources are available.
Effort is estimated in working days and represents the approximate amount of development time required for each user story.

8. User Stories

User Story 1: Anonymous Account
As a student, I want to create an account with an anonymous display name so that I can use the platform without showing my real identity to other students.
This function will allow the system to identify registered users internally while displaying only an automatically generated nickname publicly.
Priority: 10. Estimated effort: 3 days.
User Story 2: Create an Anonymous Post
As a student, I want to publish an anonymous post so that I can safely share my academic, social or university-life concerns.
The post will include a title, a description and a selected category. The student’s real name will not be displayed.
Priority: 10. Estimated effort: 4 days.
User Story 3: View Anonymous Posts
As a student, I want to view posts published by other students so that I can learn from their experiences and provide support.
The main page will display recent posts together with their titles, categories, anonymous usernames and publication times.
Priority: 10. Estimated effort: 3 days.
User Story 4: Anonymous Comments
As a student, I want to comment anonymously on another student’s post so that I can provide encouragement or practical advice.
Comments will display the commenter’s anonymous nickname and will be placed below the original post.
Priority: 10. Estimated effort: 4 days.

User Story 5: Report Inappropriate Content

As a student, I want to report harmful or inappropriate posts and comments so that the platform remains safe and respectful.

The user will be able to select a reporting reason, such as harassment, discrimination, spam, misleading information or inappropriate language.

Priority: 10. Estimated effort: 3 days.

User Story 6: Administrator Moderation

As an administrator, I want to review reported posts and comments so that I can remove content that violates the community guidelines.

The administrator will be able to view the reported content, the reporting reason and the date of the report before deciding whether to keep or remove the content.

Priority: 10. Estimated effort: 5 days.

User Story 7: Post Categories

As a student, I want to select a category when creating a post so that other users can understand the topic of my post.

The initial categories will include academic pressure, friendship, internship and career, campus life, and general support.


User Story 8: Search and Filter Posts

As a student, I want to search and filter posts so that I can quickly find discussions related to my needs.

Students will be able to search by keywords and filter posts according to programme-related or general support categories.

Priority: 20. Estimated effort: 4 days.

User Story 9: Supportive Reactions

As a student, I want to use supportive reactions so that I can encourage another student without writing a complete comment.

The initial reactions will include “I understand”, “You are not alone” and “Sending support”.

Priority: 20. Estimated effort: 2 days.

User Story 10: University Support Resources

As a student, I want to access official university support information so that I know where to obtain appropriate assistance.

The resource page will contain information about student services, academic support, counselling services and relevant university contact channels.

Priority: 20. Estimated effort: 2 days.

User Story 11: Edit and Delete Personal Posts

As a student, I want to edit or delete my own post so that I can correct mistakes or remove information that I no longer wish to share.

Only the original author of the post and an authorised administrator will be able to edit or remove it.

Priority: 30. Estimated effort: 3 days.

User Story 12: Save Useful Posts

As a student, I want to save useful posts so that I can easily read them again later.

Saved posts will be displayed in a private section of the student’s account and will not be visible to other users.

Priority: 30. Estimated effort: 3 days.

User Story 13: Reply Notifications

As a student, I want to receive a notification when another user replies to my post or comment so that I do not miss new responses.

The notification will show that a response has been received without revealing unnecessary personal information.

Priority: 30. Estimated effort: 4 days.

User Story 14: Anonymous Private Messaging

As a student, I want to send an anonymous private message to another student so that I can continue a supportive conversation privately.

The private-message function will protect both users’ identities and will include blocking and reporting options.

Priority: 40. Estimated effort: 6 days.

User Story 15: Dark Mode

As a student, I want to switch the platform to dark mode so that it is more comfortable to use in low-light environments.

The setting will allow users to switch between light mode and dark mode according to their preferences.

Priority: 50. Estimated effort: 2 days.

9. Conclusion

The target-user research indicates that students face to different types of pressure, but they share several common requirements.
The most important requirements are anonymous posting, privacy protection, anonymous comments, content reporting and administrator moderation. These functions have therefore been assigned Priority 10 and should be developed first.
Post categories, searching, supportive reactions and university support resources are also important, but they can be developed after the essential platform functions have been completed.
Features such as private messaging and dark mode have lower priority because they are not necessary for the first working version of Hello Dear.
Overall, the identified requirements and user stories provide a clear foundation for the design and development of the Hello Dear anonymous student peer support platform.
